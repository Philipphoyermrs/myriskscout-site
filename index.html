How we calculate risk scores

MyRiskScout uses advanced machine-learning models to estimate how crime risk differs across neighbourhoods. The model learns from millions of historic crime cases and hundreds of environmental features â€“ such as housing types, distance to main roads, population density, income levels and mobility patterns.

Each neighbourhood receives a score between 1 and 100 (higher = higher relative risk). We translate these scores into clear colours so you can see the difference at a glance:

  • Green: Low risk
  • Yellow: Medium risk
  • Red: High risk
